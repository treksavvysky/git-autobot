Welcome to the Pacific Crest Trail

The Pacific Crest Trail (PCT) is one of the most iconic long-distance hiking trails in the world, stretching over 2,650 miles from Mexico to Canada through California, Oregon, and Washington. It offers breathtaking views of diverse landscapes, from deserts to mountains, and is a must for adventure seekers.

Whether you're planning a thru-hike or a day trip, the PCT promises unforgettable experiences in nature.

About the Pacific Crest Trail

Established in 1993, the PCT traverses 25 national forests, 7 national parks, and 48 wilderness areas. Highlights include the Mojave Desert, Sierra Nevada, Cascade Range, and volcanic landscapes like Crater Lake and Mount Hood.

Best time to hike: Late spring to early fall, depending on the section, to avoid snow in higher elevations.

Hiking Tips for the PCT

  • Pack lightweight gear and plenty of water, especially in arid sections.
  • Obtain necessary permits for overnight stays in protected areas.
  • Train with elevation changes to prepare for the trail's challenges.
  • Follow Leave No Trace principles to preserve the environment.
  • Check weather forecasts and trail conditions via the Pacific Crest Trail Association website.
